📊 Tracking Digital India: AI Adoption Among Indian Students
An Interactive Power BI Analytics & Forecasting Project

🧠 The Mission
Artificial Intelligence is fundamentally shifting how Indian students approach education. This project moves beyond simple tool-tracking to explore a critical socio-economic question: How are students navigating the friction between rapid innovation and institutional resistance? By analyzing awareness, trust, and infrastructure, this dashboard identifies the barriers to equitable digital growth in India.

🔍 Core Research Questions
The Trust Gap: How does student awareness correlate with actual trust in AI?
The Tool War: Which platforms—ChatGPT, Copilot, or Gemini—are winning the Indian classroom?
Academic Friction: How does student willingness to pay for tools clash with institutional policies?
Infrastructure Barriers: To what extent is poor internet connectivity stifling adoption?

📈 Executive Insights
Based on a survey of 2,000 students across various academic streams:
Trust vs. Awareness: Awareness is moderate (5.73/10), but Trust remains low at (2.99/5).
Market Leaders: A neck-and-neck race: ChatGPT (433) leads Copilot (432) and Gemini (424).
Usage Patterns: Students spend an average of 2.64 hours/day primarily on assignments and coding.
Hardware Parity: Usage is nearly identical across Laptops (34.3%), Tablets (33.5%), and Mobile (32.2%).
Institutional Barriers: Despite 51.3% of students being ready to pay for AI, 50.95% of professors still prohibit its use.
Connectivity Crisis: Only 31.7% of students enjoy high-speed access; nearly 68% struggle with medium-to-poor connectivity.

🛠️ Technical Stack
Data Orchestration: Power BI Desktop
ETL & Cleaning: Power Query (Transforming raw survey data)
